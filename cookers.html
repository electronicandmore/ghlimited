Gas Cooker Buying Guide

When choosing a gas cooker, consider these important factors to find the perfect fit for your kitchen:

Cooker Type

Choose between free-standing models (complete cookers with oven), table-top burners (portable and space-saving), or built-in options (for customized kitchens).

Number of Burners

Consider how many dishes you typically cook simultaneously. Options range from single burners to models with 6+ burners for larger families.

Safety Features

Look for automatic ignition, flame failure devices that cut off gas if the flame goes out, and child safety locks for peace of mind.

Materials

Stainless steel bodies are durable and easy to clean. Look for cast iron or brass burners for longevity and tempered glass tops for elegant appearance.

Important Safety Tips for Gas Cookers

  • Always ensure proper ventilation when using a gas cooker
  • Have your gas connections professionally installed and checked annually
  • If you smell gas, turn off the supply, open windows, avoid electrical switches, and call a professional immediately
  • Keep flammable materials away from open flames
  • Never leave cooking unattended, especially with high flames
  • Clean burner heads regularly to prevent blockages
