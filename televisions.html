Television Buying Guide

With so many options available, choosing the right TV can be overwhelming. Here are key factors to consider when making your selection:

Screen Size

Consider your viewing distance and room size. As a rule of thumb, measure the distance from your seating area to the TV in inches, then divide by 2 to get the ideal screen size in inches.

Resolution

4K (3840 x 2160 pixels) is now standard for most TVs and offers excellent clarity. Consider 8K only for very large screens or if you're a tech enthusiast, as content is still limited.

Display Technology

OLED offers perfect blacks and wide viewing angles but can be pricier. QLED and LED provide excellent brightness and value. Mini-LED is a newer technology offering improved contrast at a midrange price.

Smart Features

Most modern TVs are "smart" with built-in streaming capabilities. Consider which platform (Android TV, webOS, Tizen, Roku) has your preferred apps and user experience.

HDR (High Dynamic Range)

HDR technology expands the range between the darkest and brightest parts of an image, providing more detail and realism. Look for formats like HDR10, HDR10+, or Dolby Vision for the best experience.

Refresh Rate

For smooth motion, especially in fast-moving content like sports or action movies, look for TVs with higher refresh rates (120Hz vs standard 60Hz). This is also important for gaming.

Sound Quality

Many slim TVs have limited audio capabilities. Consider TVs with Dolby Atmos support or budget for a separate soundbar or speaker system for a better audio experience.

Connectivity

Ensure your TV has enough HDMI ports for all your devices (gaming consoles, soundbars, etc.). For gamers, look for HDMI 2.1 support which enables 4K at 120Hz and other gaming features.
